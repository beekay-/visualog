Questions and answers

What is Moves?

The Moves mobile application uses your Android device or iPhone to record movement activity.

What is moves.husk.org?

This is a third-party site that uses the Moves API to let you view and export your Moves data. The export format is GeoJSON.

What is GeoJSON, and why should I care?

GeoJSON is a standard for specifying geographic data within a JSON file. JSON itself is "a lightweight data-interchange format" that "is easy for humans to read and write." In other words, exporting GeoJSON means you have a copy of your data that you can read, manipulate, and place onto maps.

What will you do with my Moves data?

moves.husk.org is only an intermediary for your data. It's held in memcache (a transient data store) for up to a day, but otherwise the data isn't stored. In addition, Moves only identifies users by an arbitrary ID, so moves.husk.org doesn't know who you are, and your access token for the site is stored as a cookie on your machine, only used by moves.husk.org for the duration of your page request.

Can I see how the site is written?

Sure. moves.husk.org is a Python app running Flask, and the source is available on github.
